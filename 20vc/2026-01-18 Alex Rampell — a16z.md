# Alex Rampell — a16z

> GP @ a16z | 20VC Interview | 2026-01-18

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The D**th of the Middle
In most asset classes, you need to be a large generalist or a small specialist. It is increasingly hard to be a mid-sized generalist. You will lose to both.

### 2. The Best Founders Are All Students of History
The best entrepreneurs I’ve met have learned everything about their space: Patrick Collison and John Collison: Met with Visa founder Dee Hock Vlad Tenev: Studied the history of payments Apoorva Mehta: Met with the founders of Webvan Brian Chesky: Studied B&B and hotel history going back to the 1800s This is a very classic trait.

### 3. The Best Founders Want More Than Money; They Want Revenge
Like the Count of Monte Cristo, the best founders do not care about wealth They just want to destroy their enemies & conquer the world. A lot of the best entrepreneurs have a chip on their shoulder & want to prove that they’re better than everyone else.

### 4. The Two Types of Deals That You Want to Do
You have to do one of the two: Buy any percentage of something that is absolutely working. Have a high ownership of something that could work:

### 5. The Best Companies Have Hostages Not Customers
The best enterprise tools make switching nearly impossible. If the rate of new company creation is high enough, those new companies will pick the best product. That is where startups have a shot.

### 6. Single Best Investment Advice
Find high agency people that know the history of the space, driven by revenge, and don’t second guess anything. Give them money & be their best partner. It is entirely about people.

### 7. Where Is Venture Capital in Five Years’ Time?
It will end up eating even more of the world. AI is allowing software companies to do so many things it couldn’t before. I am bullish on the ability of tech companies to create enduring value.
