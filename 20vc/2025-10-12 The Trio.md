# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2025-10-12

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Sam Altman Is the Master Power Broker
He has more power than AMD, so he takes 10% of the company for the privilege of selling to OpenAI. He has less power than Nvidia, so he gives them equity for the privilege of selling to OpenAI. Paul Graham was right: Sam Altman understands power.

### 2. The Mega Leverage That OpenAI Has Despite Being Unprofitable
OpenAI’s leverage is user demand, not profits. Vendors like Nvidia & AMD believe OpenAI’s revenue will grow from $12BN to $200BN, and that it takes $100BN/year in chips to do it. Selling to OpenAI is worth 10% of your company.

### 3. VC Is the Most Forgiving Asset Class When It Comes to Price
In PE, if you overpay by 50%, you are toast. This is the same case with public markets. In venture, you have exponential growth on your side.

### 4. The 4 Steps Companies Need to Take to Control Their Own Destiny
Incentives: Make sure your founding team is excited to keep building value. Profitability: If you’re $100M+ with high gross margins, get to profit fast. Second act: Build a real second product, ideally tied to AI/agents where relevant. Independence: With energy, profit, and a second act, you control outcomes, not acquirers or markets.

### 5. What Does It Take to IPO in 2026?
There have been 15 IPOs up to date. The median revenue run rate is ~$931M, growing around 30%. The cutoff is around $200M-300M.

### 6. How Legora Triumphed Over Harvey: David vs Goliath
Harvey had mindshare, capital, and Sequoia-backed momentum. Legora shipped fast from Sweden, broke a monopoly narrative, and won Benchmark & Bessemer. If there are two kingmakers in legal AI, there is no single king.
