# Winston Weinberg — Harvey

> Co-Founder & CEO @ Harvey | 20VC Interview | 2026-01-26

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Are We Seeing a Plateau in Model Performance?
We are seeing a plateau in consumer use cases, but we do not need better model performance. We need better context: Models need to integrate into your apps, calendar, workflows. It’s a different story for enterprise: We will not see a plateau in code gen anytime soon.

### 2. The Two Stages to Successful Company Scaling
Companies are growing much faster than they used to & they need to go through a couple stages: Stage one: Product-market fit. Stage two: Company-market fit. Company-market fit: Are you building using proven SaaS basics, while adapting to what’s different about your market?

### 3. Biggest Piece of Advice to Founders on Fundraising?
Start six months earlier. Have a few VCs put money in & keep them updated. VCs care most when founders set a goal & deliver on it. Once you hit enough milestones, the fundraising process will be much easier.

### 4. Movement Is Not Action, Read People: #1 Advice for Deals
Too many people think if they speak the most, they are in control of the deal. You need to listen more than you speak. The reality is all deal-making is just people reading.

### 5. Know When to Not Negotiate in a Deal: #2 Best Advice for Deal-Making
There are certain deals where you want one thing from the deal & nothing else matters. If you understand the value better than anyone else, throw all your deal making principles out the window. Get the one thing that is more valuable than anything else done.

### 6. Is It Total BS That All These AI Researchers Are Jumping From Company to Company?
Researchers care about working on really hard and interesting problems. Sometimes the leaders of the companies change the company direction & they are no longer interested. They have insanely high demand right now, so they’re able to pick what projects they want to work on.

### 7. Founders Must Constantly Put Pressure on Themselves
You need to constantly apply pressure & have that ownership mentality. If you don’t, you will start to get weak. That will trickle down to the rest of the company. Behind the scenes:
