# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2025-11-23

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Why 30BN for Cursor Is a Good Deal
They have huge revenue, strong revenue growth, and a large TAM. They went from $1M-$100M a year ago, then $100M-$1BN this year. Newton’s laws of motion require it to go to $3-4BN next year, which will be 10x NTM revenue.

### 2. Who Will Be the Top 3 in AI Coding?
Cursor: They are first & ahead. GitHub: They can bundle with Microsoft Anthropic: They can bundle at the model level.

### 3. Which AI Products Will Have Churn vs Not
In SaaS, the number one predictor of retention is the number of integrations. If the product is easily interchangeable, you will rip it out if it is cheaper. If the product is integrated into 5+ things & you have to speak to IT, you won’t bother.

### 4. The Late-Stage Business Is either the Best or the Worst
When prices go up, you are easily turning $100M to $200M with little to no effort on your side. When prices go down, it is the worst business in the world. There is nothing you can do to determine which one it is.

### 5. How Oracle Shows Why the Bubble Could Pop
Oracle is borrowing money to build data centres as part of their deal with OpenAI. They have 3 times the credit default swap spreads of the federal government, Microsoft, and other major tech companies. The risk is still small but it suggests a meaningful repricing of risk.

### 6. A Crash Is Coming and These Are the Data Points That Show It
Oracle’s CDS are three times Amazon and Microsoft’s. Subprime borrowers have hit the highest auto-loan delinquency rates in the past 60 days in recorded history. Blue Owl has frozen redemptions for a non-traded BDC vehicle.
