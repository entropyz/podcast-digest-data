# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2026-04-05

## Key Takeaways

### 1. What Multiple Should Oura and Whoop Get When They Go Public, Given the Questionable ARR Nature?
Oura and Whoop face “faddishness” and consumer volatility that makes their ARR less durable than enterprise SaaS. Their public multiples will hinge on whether they can prove they aren’t just another Peloton—a demand spike that eventually saturates.

### 2. Why the “Oh We Love Hardware Because It’s So Defensible” Is Such Bullshit
The VC narrative that hardware is now the only “defensible” asset is a faddy waste of time for entrepreneurs. Real success comes from conforming to customers and maintaining a “damn good R&D function” to survive high product volatility.

### 3. The Agentic Era: Accelerated Mistakes and a Cybersecurity Golden Age
The “agentic era” is a “Golden Age” for cybersecurity because agents ship “vibe code” faster than humans, leading to 100 times more mistakes. This explosion of threats makes any security company protecting organizations from autonomous agents an immediate rocket ship.

### 4. Strategic Retreats: Optimizing Scarce Compute Over “Cool” Products
Killing Sora to pivot toward ads shows that leaders must now optimize scarce compute for high-revenue functions over “cool” but expensive projects. To hit trillion-dollar caps, AI companies must focus on coding or build $20 billion ad machines.

### 5. Valuation Gamification: The Optics Trap of Tranched Rounds
Tranched rounds allow startups to “gamify” valuations, with headline unicorn prices often masking a lower average basis. This “cool tax” for follower investors creates dangerous optics that could trigger a down-round if growth falters.

### 6. Humbling Realities: Debunking the VC “Value Add” Myth
The “value add” myth is often a VC delusion, as founders handling a million tasks rarely remember the “transformative” advice VCs brag about. Founders remember who funded them during a crisis, while most other VC contributions are just bit parts in the drama.
