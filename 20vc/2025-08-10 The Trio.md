# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2025-08-10

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The People Who Said They Left Money on the Table Are Idiots
The $98 price only happened because the IPO happened at $38. If they priced it at $100 & raised at $80 it would not have worked. There would have been no demand for the shares.

### 2. Why a Direct Listing Would Not Have Been the Answer
If Figma did a direct listing they would not have listed at $100/share. They would have done the same analysis & listed at $39-40/share. These pops are like a natural phenomenon. Direct listing does not solve that part of the problem.

### 3. Why CEO Comp Is Broken and How PSUs Fix It
CEO comp packages are usually peer-based: Dylan would make ~$20M a year. That is <0.3% of his entire net worth. It does not move the needle. Having PSUs would realign incentives.

### 4. Being Public Is Not as Scary as People Think
When we were private, we had quirky, slightly misaligned VCs who were in our shorts. When we went public, we had quirky, slightly misaligned public investors who were less in our shorts. When you communicate your vision well, public investors are pretty rational & will stick with you.

### 5. Canva Should Go Public
The market’s wide open, valuations are good. It is very seasonal & timing matters. I would be lining everything up to go public.

### 6. Cognition Clearly Not Buying the Team
They bought a brand & $80M of revenue to maintain. Offering to let every single employee go means they don’t see a lot of value in who’s left.
