# Hemant Taneja — General Catalyst

> CEO @ General Catalyst | 20VC Interview | 2025-09-28

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Anthropic Was the Cheapest Round of the Year
Anthropic raised capital at 20x ARR. Everyone else is raising at 50x ARR. Risk adjusted, that was the best price you could have done it at.

### 2. Why Anthropic Investors Will Do Better Than OpenAI Investors Because of Their Capital-Efficient Strategy
OpenAI is making bets across multiple markets; Anthropic is more focused. Anthropic’s leadership is maturing from researchers to world-class company builders. Execution matters & They are launching impressive products with capital efficiency.

### 3. Second Mover Advantage in a World of AI
For the first time every CEO in every company is thinking about how to adopt AI. Second movers have better tech & clearer propositions. Early mover advantage is not guaranteed in AI.

### 4. Companies Need to Do These 4 Things to Implement AI Effectively
Data infrastructure: You need the right data infrastructure to adopt AI correctly. Models: You need to train models that understand your business. Workforce: You need to rethink your org chart; some humans will manage AI, some AI will manage humans. Commitment: The execs at the top really need to get behind it. You need all 4 for AI to truly transform your business.

### 5. How Do We Make 90% of Our Employees Be Agents?
A CEO from a large consulting firm had a big client come to him They said “We have 50,000 employees today. Make a plan for us to have 100,000 in 5 years – of which 90,000 are AI agents.” People are thinking about this already. The potential for large org changes in the next 10-15 years is not zero.

### 6. Why as a VC You Should Be Losing Deals and That Is a Good Sign
In venture, losing deals means you’re competing for the best founders. A ~30% win rate shows you’re in the right fights. If you never lose, you’re fishing in the wrong pond.

### 7. My Biggest Lesson From Losing Billions by Missing Coinbase
You should not be industry-focused at seed. I passed on the industry: I did not understand how big crypto would become. I should have focused on backing extraordinary founders & Brian Armstrong is one of them. Behind the scenes:
