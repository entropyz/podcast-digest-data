# David George — a16z

> GP & Head of Growth Fund @ a16z | 20VC Interview | 2025-12-21

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Idea That Large Funds Cant Have Great Returns Is BS
Our best performing fund ever was a $1BN Fund. Databricks returned 7x the fund. Coinbase returned 5x the fund. Github, Digital Ocean and Lyft are also in that fund. So  the idea that large funds can’t have great returns, it’s just not true.

### 2. So Much Value Is to Be Had at the Late Stage.
We looked at the top 50 IPOs from 2017 to 2025. 47% of dollar gains came from Seed to Series B, while 53% came from Series C & later stages. There is a tremendous amount of value in the later stages.

### 3. What Is the Biggest Advantage of Staying Private for Longer for a Company?
The biggest benefit: You avoid stock price volatility you get from public markets. If you can steadily grow or control your stock price in the private markets. Even if it’s a slight discount to where you would be in the public markets, it is a benefit. Companies like SpaceX, Stripe, and Databricks have been able to do that.

### 4. Does Revenue Matter as Much as It Did Before, Given How Fast and Transient It Is?
It still matters but the bar is raised: We also look for high retention & engagement. AI companies are growing so fast, we cannot look at years of renewal behavior. Heavy organic customer acquisition and high retention & engagement are all extremely good signs.

### 5. Do Margins Matter Less Than Ever in This AI World?
The history of technology inputs would suggest that the margins will rationalize & will go up. It is possible  that this next generation of companies is 50% gross margins, but if they’re delivering a ton of value growing really fast, that’s totally fine. We give more of a pass than we used to.

### 6. Why a16z Invested $300M into Adam Neumann and Flow
We invest behind strengths of strengths & Adam has some of the strongest strengths of anybody in the market. He’s got an incredible insight…The average renter in the US spends 30% of their disposable income on rent, and yet it’s the only unbranded experience in anyone’s life. The likelihood that Adam can build a huge company compared to the average entrepreneur is extremely high.
