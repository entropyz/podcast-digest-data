# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2026-03-29

## Key Takeaways

### 1. How Does TeraFab Extend the Starlink Vision and What Does That Do to DCF Models?
Elon Musk’s TeraFab vertical integration aims to support massive data center needs for Tesla and SpaceX. Given Starlink’s 53% profit margins, extending this vision five orders of magnitude justifies a $2 trillion DCF valuation. These “step-function” technical wins create massive valuation lifts that software companies rarely achieve.

### 2. If You Cannot Double Your ARPU, Your AI Is Not Very Good
The new litmus test for AI is driving ARPU up by at least 50%. Failing this means you’ve built a feature, not a transformative product. Notion passed this test by effectively doubling ARPU through deep AI-driven utility.

### 3. Who the Hell Is Going to Buy All These Billion-Dollar-Plus Companies When They Are Not Going to IPO?
We are seeing a career-low ratio of acquirers to unicorns. High valuations have outstripped hyperscalers’ M&A capacity, forcing many firms to go “all in” on IPOs. Without a functional IPO window, great companies risk entering a “dead zone” with zero exit paths.

### 4. The Marginal AI Buyer Has Shifted Toward Anthropic
The “marginal buyer” is shifting toward Claude, with Anthropic now capturing 73% of new AI spending. OpenAI’s inconsistent messaging and pivot away from hardware suggest a loss of their once-invincible aura.

### 5. The Trap of the Installed Base Can Imperil Agentic Growth
An existing installed base can trap giants like Figma, as legacy support consumes 98% of engineering resources. This prevents focusing on the “agentic” products needed to survive the AI era. Products failing to disrupt what they build risk being actively shorted by the market.

### 6. Investors Are Incentivizing a Generation of Fraud Through Growth Pressure
VCs are incentivizing young founders to artificially jack up growth to meet extreme “triple-triple” expectations. This leads to ethical shortcuts, like the Delve controversy where founders compromised honor for million-dollar deals. If a compliance timeline seems “too good to be true,” it usually is.

### 7. Billionaires Are Seeking Hospitality, Not Just Tax Breaks
Billionaires are moving to feel “welcomed” rather than vilified by local culture. While the Bay Area treats entrepreneurs like villains, regions like Miami and Utah treat them as heroes. This social discomfort drives them to take their massive capital elsewhere.
