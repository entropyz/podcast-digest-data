# Andrew Ng — AI Fund

> Managing General Partner @ AI Fund | 20VC Interview | 2025-11-23

## Key Takeaways

### 1. I Have Never Met an AI Person Who Think They Have Enough Compute
Get us any amount of compute, we will use it all & say we still don’t have enough. We have excess demand for inference. We do not have enough chips & power to meet that demand.

### 2. Models as a Source of Geopolitical Influence
AI models shape global views when people ask about borders, history or politics. They will become a new form of soft power like K-pop in South Korea or Hollywood in the US. China’s low cost, open-weight models will give them this advantage.

### 3. How Export Controls Are Chips Massively Back Fired
China’s semiconductor industry was moving slowly. Export controls on NVIDIA & AMD incentivized them to accelerate their semiconductor development. It is paying off for China.

### 4. If I Had One Wish for European Regulators
Stop regulating AI so much and focus on building. We are still in the early days of AI. Europe has plenty of talent who are willing to work hard. Being leaders in AI regulation is not a competitive advantage.

### 5. AI Means You Do Not Need to Learn Coding Is the Worst Advice Ever
Telling people not to learn coding because of AI will age very badly. If AI makes coding easier, then more people should learn to code. Those who understand code will have unparalleled advantage in most job functions.

### 6. CS Students Who Don’t Know AI Are the Most F**ked of All
The 4 tiers of software engineers today: Engineers with 10+ years of experience & understands AI. Fresh college grads who understand AI. Engineers with 10+ years of experience but do not use AI. Fresh college grads who don’t understand AI. If you are #4, you are f**ked in the job market.

### 7. Do Margins Matters in a World of AI
They are absolutely important. But when you have a clear view of where the technology is going, you are building for the margins of the future. That is an important distinction.
