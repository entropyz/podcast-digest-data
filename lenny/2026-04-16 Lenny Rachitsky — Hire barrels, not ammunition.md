Kind: captions Language: en Most companies raise money and then they hire a lot of people infallibly and then the CEO almost without exception gets frustrated because they've hired a lot of people, their burn rate has increased a lot and they don't feel like more is getting accomplished per unit of time. The fundamental driver of this is the number of people that can independently drive an initiative from inception to success is very limited within any company and if you hire more people without expanding the number of what I call barrels that can drive from inception to success, all you're doing is stacking people behind the same initiatives. You're wasting time, energy and increasing your collaboration tax. What helps you understand if someone is truly a barrel? &gt;&gt; Can they take an idea and make it happen? One way or the other, they're going to get your company across that hill. That's a barrel. Anything less than that is not a barrel. If you want to do more or need to do more, your market requires you to do more, your sales require you to do more, you need to have more barrels.
