# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2026-02-01

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Does the Brex Deal Prove Ramp to Be Dramatically Overvalued?
Brex sold to Capital One for $5.15B, about 7x revenue. Ramp’s $32BN valuation implies 30x, betting on faster, profitable growth. It doesn’t prove overvaluation, but it may put a cap on IPO multiples.

### 2. Does the Brex Exit Help or Hurt Ramp?
It is a scoreboard moment for Ramp – they look like the category winner. However, people could start questioning your 30x multiple vs Brex’s 7x. Now, a well-funded public company competitor is directly entering your space with a structural cost advantage.

### 3. You Have to Spend on Inference or You Will Be Crushed.
Everything will get more expensive. As soon as we figure out how this stuff works, we will burn an infinite amount of tokens. It is hard to stay competitive without spending more on inference.

### 4. Inference Costs Are Going Up Not Down
The people who aren’t close to AI in the company are mismodeling the costs. Inference costs will be going up this year. They will not be going down anytime soon.

### 5. Your Inference Costs Are Your Marketing Costs.
Established players don’t have that luxury. They are already spending massive amounts on traditional sales & marketing. Throw all your money into making the agent great.
