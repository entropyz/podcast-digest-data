# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2025-10-19

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Diversification in AI Investing Is the Same as Saying I Don’t Know
Those who know the winners will concentrate their capital. Those who don’t will diversify their bets to bide time. It’s better to bide time than to sit out completely.

### 2. Whenever a Tier One Firm Does an Investment in Your Company, Double Down With the Pro Rata
When a reputable outside investor does a  follow on round at a high price, do everything you can to be in it. There is a lot of signal in that. Don’t anchor to entry price; update for progress and outside validation.

### 3. The Most Valuable Venture Firms Are the Ones You Can Actually Sell
Most venture firms are not businesses: they’re collections of individuals. If you remove the partners, there’s no residual value. No IP, no moat, no brand. But when you build an institutionalized, process-driven platform (like Industry Ventures), you create something acquirable.

### 4. In AI, Your Downside Isn’t Valuation: It’s Founders Leaving
One co-founder of a $10BN AI startup left for a $3.5B Meta package. No misconduct. Just better personal economics. For early-stage investors, founder loyalty, not preferences or terms, is the real risk.
