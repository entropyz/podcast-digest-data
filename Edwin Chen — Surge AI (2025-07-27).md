# Edwin Chen — Surge AI

> Founder & CEO @ Surge AI | 20VC Interview | 2025-07-27

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The V1 for the Billion-Dollar Company and How It All Started.
I’ve worked in this space for a long time and had a clear vision on what to build. I built V1 in a couple of weeks instead of waiting to fundraise & hire engineers. There was already a giant demand.

### 2. Why Are Our Competitors are Just Human Body Shops With No Technology?
They do not have any algorithms to measure the quality of data they’re producing. They hire anybody with a CS PhD & pass them along to AI companies. They are body shops masquerading as a technology company.

### 3. The One Principle That This $30 Billion Company Will Not Let Slip.
Quality is the most important thing. We will say no to a project if we cannot guarantee the quality. It is more important than anything else.

### 4. Did Scales Acquisition Lead to a Tidal Wave in Scales Customers Coming to Surge?
We are already the biggest & best in this space. Even if teams at larger companies weren’t working with us, they already knew who to turn to.

### 5. Silicon Valley Is Full of Tourists That Have the Wrong Incentives
It is a status game for most people. People are raising not because they want to build a product that solves an idea they fundamentally believe in. They are raising so they can tell their friends they raised $10M and get a TechCrunch headline.

### 6. Why Synthetic Data Is Not All It Is Cracked up to Be, and How That Impacts the Future of Human Labelled Data.
Models trained heavily on synthetic data are only good at benchmark-style problems & terrible at real-world use cases. We’ve heard from a lot of companies that trained on synthetic data for a year before realising all the problems it’s caused. A couple thousand pieces of high-quality human-labelled data is worth more than 10 million pieces of synthetic data.

### 7. Have the Biggest Model Providers Been Created Today, or Will More Be Made in the Future?
No, there could be more powerful model developers emerging in the coming years. We are still miles away from AGI. There could still be extremely creative breakthroughs that nobody is expecting.
