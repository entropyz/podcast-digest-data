# Byron Deeter — Bessemer Venture Partners

> Partner @ Bessemer Venture Partners | 20VC Interview | 2025-08-31

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Does AI Hurt or Help Incumbents:
AI gives incumbents advantages they didn't have in cloud. The incumbents have platform advantage, data advantage and massive distribution advantages. Fast moving incumbents are gonna make a run at being the leaders in the next cycle, which hurts the challengers, and that is a reason to be scared. I still believe the high execution challengers will beat them over time.

### 2. Is Triple Triple Double Double BS Now in SaaS
Companies that hit $100M in 4 years are called “Shooting Stars”. So yes, triple, triple, double, double, is no longer exciting enough.

### 3. What No One Understands About the Unit Economics of Foundation Models
The math does pencil out when it comes to their unit economics. The P&L doesn't match. You should think of these model releases as a product in and of themselves, and there's a healthy life cycle to that product. You're monetizing last year's training in this year's revenue line while you're investing in next year's model, which is gonna be monetized in next year's release. There are these order magnitude step functions where each atomic unit of product is highly, successful and profitable. And yet the P&L looks upside down.

### 4. Will IPO Markets Open Again:
Discussions are heating up again. We are going to have a healthier IPO market at the end of this year and going into 2026. I hope the public markets return to premium multiples and the private markets price off of those. I'm not convinced we will, but I am optimistic that we are finally gonna see more IPO activity.

### 5. Should Canva Go Public?
They should and they will. I put them in the very short term bucket; likely Q3. But they could have been public a long time ago and are in no rush. It's one of our largest investments in our history. We're hundreds of millions in and we're thrilled to be part of it.

### 6. The Single Biggest Reason We Lost Money: We Got TAM Wrong
The mistake we made time and time again; we underestimated TAM and weren't sure these could be $50 billion businesses. We left a lot of money on the table. We owned 28% of Shopify and Twilio at IPO and we owned much less than Procore and ServiceTitan. We did not see how large the TAM would be.
