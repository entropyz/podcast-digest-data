# The Trio

> Market Commentary | 2025-11-30

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Signal That a Company Is in War Mode
The real signal of war mode is wondering if the CEO pushes too hard. At the best companies, CEOs sometimes snap because their drive to win is relentless. Using simple metaphors like “war mode” is what motivates 5,000 people to move in one direction.

### 2. How to Justify the $10BN Price for Sierra
The math to make this work: Sierra went from $10M last year to $100M this year. Assume they grow 5x next year, then 3x, then 2x… they reach $5B in 5 years. Service Cloud does about $8B in revenue today. The only way this math works is if they capture part of the $200B/year services market.

### 3. The Rate Limiter for Sierra and Enterprise Companies
Sierra’s growth is not constrained by demand but by enterprise change management. Each $10M rollout needs complex integrations, training and an internal process overhaul. The real rate limiter is the physics of enterprise adoption.

### 4. Either Wix Is Dramatically Undervalued, or Lovable Is Dramatically Overvalued.
If Wix pulls off their strategy, they will be valued for it. If they just check the AI box and don’t make it core to what they’re doing, Lovable will be king.

### 5. If an AI Product Needs Your Credit Card Before You Start, It Is a Scam.
The best AI companies let you try it for free before you need to pay. Lovable, Suno, ChatGPT… If you have to enter your credit card details immediately, it is a bad sign.

### 6. Why You Can Never Push Your Team Too Hard
My number one bit of advice: do not go easy on your team. Love them, back them up where they’re good, but push them even harder. The best ones will always step up. Behind the scenes:
