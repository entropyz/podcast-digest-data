# Anish Acharya — a16z

> GP @ a16z | 20VC Interview | 2026-02-15

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Why if You Are Building in AI Today You Have to Be in SF?
There is a network effect for builders in SF. For AI, so much key info gets spread around tight networks. You can make it happen anywhere, but there is something different about giving up everything & moving to SF to make it happen.

### 2. It Is Bullsh*t That We’re Gonna Vibe Code Everything
IT spend is approximately 8-12% of enterprise spend. If you have an innovation machine with AI, why would you focus on building another ERP to save 8-12% when you can use it to optimize the remaining 90%? The idea that we’ll vibe code everything is flat out wrong.

### 3. Agents Are Reducing the Switching Costs of Large Legacy Providers
Switching legacy enterprise providers used to be extremely expensive and risky. With coding agents, the complexity of switching providers will be dramatically reduced. It’s a positive incentive for the entire ecosystem.

### 4. Who Will Win in a World of AI: Incumbents or Startups?
Incumbents will make their product better for their existing categories. Microsoft will make a better word processor, Google will make a better search engine… Startups will win in native categories that did not exist before the product cycle.

### 5. Why Are We Not in an AI Bubble Now?
Demand is matching new supply, not lagging it. Prices are rising, not collapsing. Subsidies exist, but they are strategic subsidies by big tech and AI labs. 6. Why We Should Be Skeptical of a Chat-Based UI and Browse-Based Is Preferable Chat-based UIs are designed by the most high agency people in the world who want to save time. Most people do not want to save time; they want to spend time & browse. Browse-based UI will still dominate.

### 6. How Should We Change How We Think About Margin as Investors Today?
When there is a lot of organic traffic, I would look at their M1 as traffic. I would look at the margin cost of free-trials as CAC. I would  look at the margin profile of people who convert as a sort of durable margin profile of the business.

### 7. Lessons From Marc Andreessen on Process Versus Outcome
Marc’s advice: “Just be right a lot.” Your process does not matter as long as you are consistently winning. The quality of being right supersedes the why or the how. Behind the scenes:
