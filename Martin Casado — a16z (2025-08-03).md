# Martin Casado — a16z

> Partner @ a16z | 20VC Interview | 2025-08-03

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Biggest Sin Is Zero Sum Thinking
There are so many questions about whether a layer has value, margins, or is defensible. Every layer has gained value. The one sin is not playing the game.

### 2. Have All the Foundation Model Winners Already Been Founded or World of Many More
We will have new models with different flavours. We are still early: Google was a 3rd gen search engine, Facebook was a 3rd gen social network. There’s a lot of change to come.

### 3. How Will Value Disperse in New AI Markets, and How Important Is Brand Recognition in Establishing Dominance?
The markets are so large & growing so fast, we are seeing brand effects emerge. Whoever becomes a household name will get the adoption. Market leaders will have brand monopolies until the market slows down.

### 4. App Providers Have a Unique Opportunity to Create Models Where LLM Providers Don’t
Model scaling approaches aren’t generalized. Models that are good at coding may not be good at other things. App providers have an opportunity to build their own models that the large models aren’t focused on.

### 5. It Is a BS Argument to Suggest These Will Always Be Negative Margin Businesses
Founders with access to relatively cheap capital need to make a trade‑off between margins and distribution during the land‑grab phase If they do not acquire users during that period, they will never be able to monetize them. Sacrificing margin for distribution is a rational decision

### 6. Why Chinese Open Source Is the Most Dangerous
They are better at it than we are. As a result, we are seeing a proliferation of Chinese open‑source models everywhere. The right way for us to respond is to fuel our open source efforts.

### 7. Founders Can Have One Mortal Enemy to Block a Competitive Investment.
I tell our portfolio founders they only get one mortal enemy. They do not get multiple. Whoever they pick, we will kill them together.
